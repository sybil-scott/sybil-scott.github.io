# Beyond Inclusion: The Importance of Accessible Web Content and Design **Contributed by Sybil Scott** ## Presentation Overview Kat Shereko of Portent presented a lot of information clearly and concisely. She covered different types of accessibility, levels of compliance according to the [Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG)](https://www.w3.org/WAI/standards-guidelines/wcag/), and best practices under several common issues, including * Headers * Images * Forms * Color * Video * Links She also offered steps for approaching accessiblity on your own website: 1. Choose a level of conformance 2. Audit one segment of your website at a time 3. Publish an accessibility statement ## Takeaways I've come across accessibility in several of my technical communications courses and it always feels like an afterthought, as if the advice is: design your website and then audit it for accessibility. What I learned from this presentation is that many of the elements of accessibility overlap with or are woven into the principles of good information design, such as using a logical header structure, having sufficiant color contrast between elements, and avoiding generic link language such as "click here." There is, therefore, no need to think in terms of "design, then audit." Considering accessiblity _while designing_ is not only more efficient than auditing afteward, but can actually aid in creating content that ensures the best experience for every user, regardless of ability/disability.
